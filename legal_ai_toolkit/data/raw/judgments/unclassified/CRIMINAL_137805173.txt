                                         ORDER

                                        30.11.2023
 Order No.

02.      1.         Heard learned counsel for the Petitioners and learned
         counsel for the State.

         2.         The Petitioner are accused in connection with T.R. Case
         No.92 of 2023, pending in the Court of learned Sessions Judge-
         cum-Special Judge, Sambalpur, arising out of EI & EB Unit-II,
         (ND) Sambalpur P.R. Case No.233 of 2023-24 for alleged
         commission of offences under 
Section 20(b)(ii)(C)
 of the NDPS
         Act.

         3.         Being aggrieved by the rejection of their application for
         bail U/s.439 
Cr.P.C
. by the learned Sessions Judge-cum-Judge
         (Special Court), Sambalpur by order dated 17.10.2023 in the
         aforementioned case, the present BLAPL has been filed.

         4.         Keeping in view the order dated 07.08.2023 passed in
         respect of the co-accused in BLAPL No.8093 of 2023, since the
         consideration of the learned Court in seisin is before charge sheet
         and considering the quantity of contraband to the tune of 40Kgs
         (Ganja) and taking into account the stipulation under 
Section 37
 of

                                                                          Page 1 of 2


NDPS Act
, this Court is not inclined to entertain the bail application
                                   at this stage without having the benefit of evaluation of
                                   incriminating materials qua the Petitioners, post charge sheet by the
                                   learned Court in seisin.

                                   5.       The Petitioners is at liberty to move the learned Court in
                                   seisin after filing of charge sheet. If such an application is moved,
                                   the same shall be considered on its own merits without being
                                   influenced by the earlier rejection or the order passed by this Court.

                                   6.       Accordingly, the BLAPL stands disposed of.
