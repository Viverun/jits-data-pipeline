1. This bunch of writ petitions arises out of judgment and order dated 22.11.2002

passed by the learned Maharashtra Administrative Tribunal [hereinafter referred to as

"the Tribunal"] in Original Applications No. 254 and 256 of 2000, and subsequent orders

passed in Contempt Petition No 89 of 2003 and other proceedings. [Petitioners herein

are referred to as 'Petitioners' and original applicants before MAT as 'The applicants" and

the Governments Authorities either as 'Authorities' or 'Respondents no. 1 to 3' for

convenience]

2. Original Application No. 254 of 2000 had been filed on 07-04-2000 by four

applicants, who are direct recruits to the Maharashtra Development Service ("MDS")

Class I cadre, against Respondents No. 1 to 3 - the government authorities and

Respondents No. 4 to 24 in said original application, who are the promotees to MDS

Class-I cadre, praying for placement of the applicants in seniority list of said cadre above

said Respondents No. 2 to 24 and for consequential benefits.

3. On 10-04-2000, applicants in Original Application No. 254 of 2000 had filed

Original Application No.256 of 2000 arraigning only aforesaid Respondents No. 1 to 3-

the govt. authorities, seeking directions to work out and declare number of posts

earmarked for direct recruits from 01-04-1982 and also to workout number of promotee

candidates considering their promotions in excess of the recruitment/quota rules to be

fortuitous in MDS Class-I cadre and to fill up the created posts of Additional Chief

Executive Officers in Zilla Parishad in accordance with quota rule.

4. According to the applicants, MDS cadre has been an altogether new cadre formed in

1973, consisting of Class-I and Class-II posts pursuant to Maharashtra Development

[Constitution, Classification and Recruitment] Rules, 1973 [hereinafter referred to as

'MDS Rules, 1973'] and the posts in said cadre were meant to be filled in by promotion as

well as by nomination with prescription that appointments of direct recruits [nomination]

shall be in the range of 35 to 50 per cent and the rest by promotion [departmental

candidates]. The MDS Rules, 1973, have been framed in exercise of powers conferred

upon the State of Maharashtra under proviso to
Article 309
of the Constitution of India.

According to them, in order to achieve proportion of 35 per cent of direct recruits till

1.4.1982 and 50 percent thereafter, and to maintain the proportion under the rules, the

State Government ought to take proper steps and timely action.

5. The applicants have submitted that sub-rule (1) of Rule 5 of the MDS Rules,

1973, requires that appointment by nomination shall be through the Commission in

accordance with the rules relating to recruitment to the posts of Deputy Collectors and

that proviso thereunder requires the same to be effective from 1.4.1979 or thereafter in

such a phased manner as would ensure that the total number of officers directly recruited

in MDS Class-I cadre shall not at any time after 1.4.1982, be less than 35 per cent and

more than 50 per cent of the total number of permanent posts in that cadre.

their representation in accordance with rules. According to them, direct recruitment

was sought only in 1987 though it was expected immediately on enforcement of the

13. When the cases were taken up for hearing before us, it was faintly suggested

unsound and fit to be over-ruled, but no attempt was made to substantiate the

more than once and we are in complete agreement with the ratio decidendi,
that the period of continuous officiation by a government servant, after his
appointment by following the rules applicable for substantive appointments, has

to be taken into account for determining his seniority; and seniority cannot be
determined on the sole test of confirmation, for, as was pointed out,
confirmation is one of the inglorious uncertainties of government services
depending neither on efficiency of the incumbent nor on the availability of
substantive vacancies. The principle for deciding inter se seniority has to conform

to principles of equality spelt out by Arts. 14 and 16. If an appointment is made
by way of stop-gap arrangement, without considering the claims of all the
eligible available persons and without following the rules of appointment, the

experience on such appointment cannot be equated with the experience of a
regular appointee, because of the qualitative difference in the appointment. To
equate the two would be to treat two unequals as equal which would violate the
equality clause. But if the appointment is made after considering the claims of

all eligible candidates and the appointee continues in the post uninterruptedly till
the regularisation of his service in accordance with the rules made for regular
substantive appointments, there is no reason to exclude the officiating service for
purpose of seniority. Same will be the position if the initial appointment itself is
made in accordance with the rules applicable to substantive appointments as in
the present case. To hold otherwise will be discriminatory and arbitrary.

in going beyond the spirit and letter of the proviso to clause (a) of sub rule (1) of Rule 5.

109. Though rival arguments have been advanced before us by petitioners so also by

the respondents that the original application was beyond limitation and per contra that it

is within limitation, we do not find it necessary to go into that aspect of the matter. The

learned Tribunal after hearing the parties has decided the matter on merits. We have

also considered the matter on merits and, as such, it would not be appropriate at this

stage to go into that issue.

110. In our considered view, therefore, the Tribunal has wholly misconceived the

scope of the matter before it had been in error all over. The impugned order cannot be

justified with the aid of material on record. The Tribunal, thus, has erred in issuing

impugned directions to prepare fresh seniority list. Looking at the matter in any way,

the impugned order passed by the Tribunal dated 22-11-2002 and the one dated

28-04-2004 are wholly unsustainable and deserve to be quashed and set aside and as its

incident, the impugned seniority list would not be able to stand.
